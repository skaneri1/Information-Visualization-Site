Retail Revenues

Physical Space? Online Presence? Porque no los dos?

Project By: Anthony Freay & Shlok Kaneria

Abstract

In this project, we begin to delve into the analysis of how different corporations have either perfected or are trying to perfect the presence of their entities in both the physical and online marketplaces. Through our findings, we attempt to prove why it is vital for companies to be successful in both sectors in order to maximize revenue. Our data consists of quarterly revenue for four of today's biggest online/physical retailers, including Walmart, Amazon, Ebay, and Target.

Visual Analysis #1: Line Graph (Static)

As depicted by this static visual, Ebay went through some soul searching and they realized that they were flushing money on google ads, so they pumped the brakes. On the contrary, Walmart decided to acquire Jet.com for $3.3B after they saw their revenues plateauing. This prompted a slight increase thereafter in their quarterly income. Amazon decided to expand into the physical marketspace by aquiring Whole Foods for dumb money ($13.7B). Target, trying to play catch up, acquired Shipt, Inc. for a measly $550M in Q4 2017.

Visual Analysis #1: Line Graph (Interactive)

This visual portrays the total revenue made available to us. As we see, the spike in revenue at Q1 shows how the holiday season has a big impact in the performance of these corporations. The interactivity of this line graph allows us to compare how the different companies have trended over time and how various acquisitions have played a role.

Conclusion

Challenges Encountered:

          Throughout this project, we encountered many challenges that we had to solve. We struggled with finding an adequate way to represent the x-axis for the interactive line graph ordinally. Often times, we ran into unexpected issues with D3 and formatting the site in general.


Things We Learned:

          This final project allowed us to gain insight into visualizing information and data effectively through various means/platforms. Specifically pertaining to this project, we wanted to emphasize the importance for corporations to have both a physical and online presence if they want to capitalize on profits and gain an advantage over competitors. Ebay's inability to do so has hindered them drastically whereas Amazon and Walmart are trailblazers in the multi-channel marketspace.
